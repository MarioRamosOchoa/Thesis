Pell Grant

Money for college from the federal government that does not need to be paid back; given to undergraduate students with financial need. For more information, go to the FAFSA guide we wrote up or by going to their website.

Perkins Loan

A federal student loan, made by the recipient's school, for undergraduate and graduate students who demonstrate financial need. Read more about it here.

Private Loan

A nonfederal loan made by a lender such as a bank, credit union, state agency, or school. These are alternatives in case your financial aid does not cover enough of your tuition. However, use them sparringly and not at all if possible. They will likely have higher interests than federal loans. Speak to your financial aid office and push for more financial aid because of your financial circumstances.

Retention Rate

The percentage of first-time students seeking bachelor’s degrees who return to the institution to continue their studies the following fall. This is a good measure as to how many students will not return to school.

Room and Board

An allowance for the cost of housing and food while attending college or career school. Some summer jobs, internships, and other programs will offer this to students. It is always nice to have if you are going to be living far away from home.

SAT

A college admission test usually taken during junior or senior year that measures students’ skills. Learn more in our ACT & SAT page.

Undergraduate

Student seeking an associate’s or bachelor’s degree.

Work Study

A federal student aid program that provides part-time employment while you are enrolled in school to help pay your education expenses.
