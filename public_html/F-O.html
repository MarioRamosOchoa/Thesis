Financial Aid

Money given to you by the school. This can be in the form of scholarships, loans, grants, work-study, and more. Talk to your financial aid office about how you can get more money for tuition.

Grace Period

For certain types of federal student loans, this is a period of time after you graduate, leave school, or drop below half-time enrollment when you are not required to make payments. You are responsible for paying the interest that accrues on unsubsidized loans during the grace period. If the interest is unpaid, it will be added to the principal balance of the loan (capitalized) when the repayment period begins.

Grant

Financial aid, often based on financial need, which does not need to be repaid.

In-State Tuition

Residents pay lower tuition at public universities and community college than residents of other state because taxpayers help support these institutions. The opposite of this is out of state tuition.

Interest (Loans)

A loan expense charged for the use of borrowed money, paid by a borrower to a lender. Interest is calculated as a percentage, or rate, of the unpaid principal amount of the loan.

Loans

Need-based financial aid that must be paid back.

Loan Repayment Plan

The schedule of how much and how often students pay back their loans. This can be changed at any time for no charge.

Major

Specialized field of study or subject are that a student chooses to study at college.

Master's Degree

An academic credential in a specific field of study that can usually be completed in about two years after getting a bachelor’s degree.

Net Price

An estimate of the actual cost that a student and their family need to pay in a given year to cover education expenses for the student to attend a particular school. Net price is determined by taking the institution’s cost of attendance and then subtracting any gift aid for which the student may receive.

Oregon Opportunity Grant

Oregon's largest state-funded need-based grant program for students planning to go to college. For more information visit their website.

Oregon Promise

A grant program that covers most of the costs for eligible students who plan to attend one of Oregon’s 17 public community colleges.

ORSAA

Oregon Student Aid Application; the form that eligible undocumented and DACA-mented students complete in order to access state financial aid including the Oregon Opportunity Grant, the Oregon Promise grant and some OSAC scholarships. Opens October 1.

OSAC

Office of Student Access and Completion; the agency in Oregon that processes applications for many different scholarships as well as the Oregon Promise program.

Out-of-State Tuition

Usually higher tuition costs than in-state tuition because you state's taxpayers are not providing funding for you to attend.
